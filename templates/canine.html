Canine Adoptions


Analysis

Statistics involving canine adoptions were examined to determine if there were any discernible patterns. Male and female adoptions were almost equal, with male adoptions having a slight lead at 54%. This suggests that both sexes were almost equally sought after for adoption. Canines of 1-3 years of age were the most commonly adopted, followed by puppies of 1-6 months. It stamds to reason that younger dogs may be more desirable. The most commonly adopted primary breeds were retriever, chihuahua, and pit bull, respectively.
